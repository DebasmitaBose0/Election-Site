Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about voting in Indian elections

You must be 18 years or older on the qualifying date (January 1st of the election year) to be eligible to vote in India.

You can register online through the National Voter Service Portal (NVSP) at voters.eci.gov.in, or visit your nearest Electoral Registration Office (ERO) with proof of identity and address.

You can locate your polling station using the Election Commission's website, mobile app, or by contacting your local electoral officer. You can also use our interactive booth finder tool.

Assembly elections are held every 5 years. The next major elections for 5 states (West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Assam, Puducherry) are scheduled for 2026. Check our timeline for exact dates.

You need your Voter ID card (EPIC) or any other approved photo identity document like Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License, etc. Some states also accept PAN cards or bank passbooks.

Yes, you can apply for a postal ballot or vote at your current location if you meet the criteria for service voters, persons with disabilities, or senior citizens over 85.

NOTA allows you to register a protest vote if you don't support any of the candidates. It's available on EVMs and counted separately from regular votes.

No, voting is not compulsory in India. However, it's your fundamental right and duty as a citizen to participate in the democratic process.

No, once you press the vote button on the EVM, your vote is recorded and cannot be changed. Make sure you vote for the right candidate.

If you suspect impersonation, you can still cast a 'Tendered Vote' after verification by the presiding officer. Report the incident to the Election Commission immediately.

Yes, Non-Resident Indians can vote in their home constituency if they maintain their voter registration. They can apply for postal ballots or vote in person during elections.

Polling stations are generally open from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. However, this can vary by state and constituency. Check local announcements for exact timings.

No, mobile phones and electronic devices are not allowed within 100 meters of polling stations to maintain election integrity.

Election results are counted at designated counting centers under supervision. EVM votes are tallied electronically, while postal ballots are counted manually. Results are announced constituency-wise.

The Model Code of Conduct is a set of guidelines that political parties and candidates must follow during elections to ensure free and fair elections. It prohibits certain activities that could influence voters unfairly.
